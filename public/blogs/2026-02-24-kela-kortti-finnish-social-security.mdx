# Kela-kortti: Your Key to Finnish Social Security

Navigating a new country can be daunting, especially when it comes to understanding the social security system. In Finland, the **Kela-kortti: Your Key to Finnish Social Security**. This card, issued by Kela (The Social Insurance Institution of Finland), is more than just a piece of plastic; it's your gateway to a wide range of social security benefits and services, including healthcare, financial aid for students, and family benefits. Let's delve into what the Kela-kortti is, how to get one, and why it's so important for anyone residing in Finland.

## Unlocking Finnish Social Security: The Kela-kortti Explained

The Kela-kortti essentially proves your eligibility for Finnish social security benefits. It's proof that you are covered for certain expenses and entitled to specific services. The most common use is at the doctor's office or pharmacy, where showing your Kela-kortti often reduces the cost of treatment and medications. But the benefits extend far beyond just healthcare. Kela provides a safety net for residents in various life situations, from unemployment to parenthood.

![Illustration of a person presenting a Kela-kortti at a doctor's office, with friendly doctors and nurses in the background.](/blogs/images/2026-02-24-kela-kortti-finnish-social-security-img1.webp)

## Who is Eligible for a Kela-kortti?

Eligibility depends on your residency status. Generally, if you live in Finland permanently, you are entitled to Kela benefits. This usually means having a municipality of residence in Finland. For those coming to Finland to work or study, the rules can be a bit more complex. Factors like the duration of your stay, your employment contract, and your citizenship all play a role. EU citizens generally have easier access to Kela benefits than non-EU citizens, thanks to agreements ensuring social security coordination. The best approach is always to check the Kela website or contact Kela directly to determine your specific eligibility.

## Applying for Your Kela-kortti

The application process itself is fairly straightforward. You'll need to fill out an application form, which can be found on the Kela website or at a Kela office. You'll also need to provide supporting documentation, such as your passport, residence permit (if applicable), and proof of address. If you're employed, you'll need to provide your employment contract. It's a good idea to gather all the necessary documents beforehand to make the application process smoother. Once your application is approved, you'll receive your Kela-kortti in the mail.
