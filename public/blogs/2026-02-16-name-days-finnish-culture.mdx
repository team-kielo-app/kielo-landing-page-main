# Name Days: More Than Just Coffee and Cake!

Name days in Finland are a beloved tradition, often celebrated with as much enthusiasm as birthdays. But **Name Days: More Than Just Coffee and Cake!** They're a window into Finnish culture, history, and even language. Let's explore this fascinating aspect of Finnish life.

## A History of Names

The tradition of name days dates back to the medieval Catholic Church, where each day was associated with a saint. When Finland converted to Lutheranism, many of these names were retained, and new ones were added. Over time, the Finnish name day calendar evolved to include names of Finnish origin and significance. Today, name days are a secular celebration, recognizing the individual and their connection to the broader cultural heritage. The Finnish name day calendar is updated periodically to include new and popular names. You can find the official list online.
