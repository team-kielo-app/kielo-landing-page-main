## The Magic of the Kokko (Bonfire)

The heart of Juhannus is the *kokko*, or Midsummer bonfire. Traditionally, these huge bonfires were believed to ward off evil spirits and guarantee a good harvest. Nowadays, the *kokko* symbolizes the burning away of the old and welcoming the new. Building and lighting the *kokko* is a communal effort, often done on islands or by lakeshores, creating a breathtaking spectacle as the flames reflect on the water. Many communities even have bonfire competitions, adding to the festive atmosphere. The bigger, the better!

## Juhannus Traditions and Superstitions

Beyond the bonfire, Juhannus is steeped in traditions and superstitions. Many believe that Midsummer night is a time of powerful magic. Single women traditionally pick seven different flowers and place them under their pillow on Juhannus night, hoping to dream of their future husband. Another popular tradition involves visiting a sauna, often followed by a refreshing swim in a lake or the sea ‚Äì a true Finnish experience! Dancing, singing, and enjoying the long hours of daylight are also essential parts of the celebration.
