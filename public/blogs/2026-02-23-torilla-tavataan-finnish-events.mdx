## Celebrating Traditions: Festivals and Holidays

Finns love to celebrate, and their calendar is filled with festivals and holidays that offer a glimpse into their cultural heritage. From Vappu (May Day), a boisterous celebration of spring and student life, to Juhannus (Midsummer), with its bonfires and lakeside gatherings, there's always something happening. Independence Day (December 6th) is a more solemn occasion, marked by parades and candlelight vigils. Each event provides a unique window into Finnish history, values, and traditions. Participating in these celebrations allows you to connect with the local community and experience the genuine warmth of Finnish hospitality.
